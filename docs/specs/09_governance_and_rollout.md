# 09. Governance and Rollout

## Positioning
SSNP should be introduced as a network-stability support program,
not as a protocol rewrite.

## Deployment Phases
Implementation may run on Symbol testnet during rollout and proving.
That does not change the mainnet-facing positioning of the program.

### Phase 1
Monitoring, registration, public ranking, and alerts only.

### Phase 2
Manual or semi-manual reward distribution from an external pool.

### Phase 3
Automated reward calculation and distribution, subject to governance approval.

## Governance Principle
Any future move toward protocol-level economic integration
must be treated as a separate governance discussion.

## Operating Responsibility
The portal must be operated by a clearly identified accountable entity.
That entity may be:

- a dedicated legal entity;
- a formal operating committee;
- or another explicitly governed body with defined authority.

The accountable operator must retain responsibility for:
- terms and policy changes;
- funding control;
- appeals and dispute handling;
- final classification decisions;
- emergency intervention.

## Comparison to the NEM Supernode Model
SSNP may adopt an operating-body structure broadly comparable to the historical NEM Supernode Program,
in the sense that a clearly identified central operator administers monitoring, publication, and reward operations.

However, SSNP should not copy that model blindly.
SSNP requires stricter governance around:

- voting-key validity and current-epoch conditions;
- Same Operator Group classification;
- appeals and re-evaluation;
- automation and AI decision boundaries.

## AI and Automation Boundary
AI agents and automation may handle:
- monitoring;
- anomaly detection;
- notification delivery;
- ranking calculation;
- reporting and issue generation.

AI agents and automation must not hold final authority over:
- fund disbursement approval;
- Same Operator Group final classification;
- operator disqualification;
- policy exceptions;
- appeal outcomes.

## Low-Ops Design Goal
SSNP should be designed for low operational overhead, not for absence of accountability.
Capital alone is not a substitute for governance.
The practical target is:

- routine operations mostly automated;
- sensitive decisions human-approved;
- all material decisions logged and reviewable.

## Communication Rule
Always frame SSNP as:
- not a harvesting reduction;
- not a protocol change;
- not a CPU-performance contest;
- not a popularity reward system.

## MVP Position
The first release should be valuable even if no reward source is approved.
If the monitoring and registration layer is useless without rewards,
the design is wrong.
